# How do I help?

Read through issues, discuss solutions, read the manuscript, background information, and finally when you have an understanding of the story, you can try to edit it and make contributions to it.

If you find any plot-holes or conflicts in the background information, create an issue. 

# How is this not going to be a disaster?

Many open source projects like this become disastors (see a [Million Penguins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Million_Penguins)).

We are not going that route by using a few very simple methods:
- if a contributer breaks the rules, they can be banned from contributing
- editors must look over and allow changes, when users can be trusted, they will be able to become editors themselves

Something to note:
Anyone, even if banned, may make their own fork of the book, which could turn into something totally different. They are not us, and that is ok. They will be able to make their own story (as long as they keep the MIT license).

# What are the rules?

- Understand at least some part of the story before trying to contribute.
- No use of AI will be permitted (it is obvious, and we will be using AI detectors).
- No insults/threats/doxing other contributers
- No gore, nsfw, or swears in main thread of the book

# What is the repository going to consist of?

The repository may have many things, from fanart, to detailed world building, to covers, to book titles, to manuscripts, and finally the official book.
